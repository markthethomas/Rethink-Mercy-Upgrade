What's happening at Rethink Mercy?

The 2014 Rethink Mercy Conference

A Call to Gospel-Centered Mercy Ministry

Rethink Mercy knows that not every church’s mercy ministry is identical. We want to help yours to be more effective, more intentional, and more gospel-centered. Rethink Mercy helps your church be effective in mercy ministry by truly helping those in need and to be gospel-centered by building relationships that are focused on evangelism and discipleship.

Explore gospel-centered mercy ministry in your church. Join us for the 2014 Rethink Mercy Conference.

When?: Friday, April 11, 2014, Immediately following the Together for the Gospel
Where?: at The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary in Louisville, Kentucky

Tentative Conference Schedule
8:00 AM Check-in
9:00 AM Dhati Lewis
10:45 AM Pastor's Roundtable
Noon Lunch (Provided)
1:30 PM Al Mohler
2:45 PM Q & A with Mohler and Lewis
3:30 PM Dismissal

Featured speakers

Dr. Albert Mohler

“The Gospel is not a message of social salvation, but it does have social implications. The church is not to adopt a social reform platform as its message, but the faithful church, wherever it is found, is itself a social reform movement precisely because it is populated by redeemed sinners who are called to faithfulness in following Christ. I look forward to the Rethink Mercy Conference where we can examine what God’s Word says about the primacy of the gospel and its implications for mercy ministry in the Church.”

-Dr. Albert Mohler


Dr. R. Albert Mohler Jr. serves as president of The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary – the flagship school of the Southern Baptist Convention and one of the largest seminaries in the world.

Pastor Dhati Lewis

“When you unleash healthy people who have been changed by the gospel, they will do ministry where life exists. In every setting-urban, suburban, and rural-physical needs exist alongside spiritual needs. As God’s people proclaim the Good News they will have the opportunity to meet some of those needs and in part demonstrate the truth of the message in their lives. The Rethink Mercy Conference will be a great opportunity to study what God’s Word says about the gospel and mercy.”

-Dhati Lewis


Dhati, who did his graduate studies at Dallas Theological Seminary (Master of Arts in Cross Cultural Ministries), is the Lead Pastor at Blueprint Church in Atlanta, GA.
